We Present The Porsche 911

\

The Porsche 911 (pronounced Nine Eleven or in German: Neunelfer) is a two-door 2+2 high performance rear-engined sports car introduced in September 1964 by Porsche AG of Stuttgart, Germany. It has a rear-mounted flat-six engine and originally a torsion bar suspension. The car has been continuously enhanced through the years but the basic concept has remained unchanged. The engines were air-cooled until the introduction of the 996 series in 1998

Engine

4.0-liter flat-six with 518 horsepower and 346 pound-feet of torque (GT3 RS) twin-turbo 3.8-liter flat-six with 543 horsepower and 442 pound-feet of torque (Sport Classic)

Peformance

The "standard" 911 sticks to its roots with a set of twin-turbo flat-six engines that have been tuned for up to 473 horsepower. Higher-performance Turbo and GT3 models are available this is Porsche, of course but we review those cars separately.
